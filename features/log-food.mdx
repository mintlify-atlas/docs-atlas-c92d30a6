The **Alimentos** screen lets you log the CO2e emissions from anything you eat. You choose a food item from the built-in database, enter the weight you consumed in grams, and the app calculates and saves the result.

## How to log a food item

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the food log">
    From the dashboard, tap the **Alimentos** button. This navigates to `/log-food`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search or select a food">
    Use the search field to filter items by name, or scroll through the list. The database contains 35+ items across multiple food categories.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the weight in grams">
    Type the amount you consumed in the weight field. The value must be a positive number in grams (e.g. `150` for 150 g of chicken breast).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Registrar actividad">
    Press **Registrar actividad** to save the entry. The app calculates emissions, writes the activity to your history, and updates the dashboard totals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Emission calculation

Every food item in the database has a pre-defined emission factor expressed in **kg CO2e per kilogram of food**. The formula applied is:

```
emissions (kg CO2e) = (emissionFactor Ă— grams) / 1000
```

**Example:** Beef has an emission factor of approximately 27 kg CO2e/kg. Logging 200 g gives:

```
(27 Ă— 200) / 1000 = 5.40 kg CO2e
```

The result is stored on the `ActivityEntry` object as the `emissions` field (a number in kg CO2e).

<Info>
  Emission factors are sourced from lifecycle assessment data and represent the full supply-chain footprint of each food, including agriculture, processing, and transport to market.
</Info>

## Food categories

The FOOD_DATABASE covers a wide range of everyday items grouped by category:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Carnes y proteĂ­nas animales">
    Includes beef, pork, chicken, lamb, turkey, and eggs. These items tend to carry the highest emission factors due to land use and methane from livestock.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="LĂˇcteos">
    Includes cow's milk, cheese, butter, and yogurt. Dairy production generates significant methane and nitrous oxide emissions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pescados y mariscos">
    Includes salmon, tuna, shrimp, and other common seafood. Emission factors vary significantly by fishing or farming method.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cereales y legumbres">
    Includes rice, wheat bread, oats, lentils, chickpeas, and tofu. Plant-based staples generally have the lowest emission factors.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Frutas y verduras">
    Includes a variety of fresh produce. Seasonal and locally grown options typically carry lower emissions than out-of-season or imported equivalents.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Bebidas">
    Includes coffee, beer, wine, and soft drinks. Processing and packaging contribute the most to beverage emissions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data saved

After you tap **Registrar actividad**, the following `ActivityEntry` is created and persisted via `addActivity()`:

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| `type` | `"food"` |
| `name` | Selected food item name |
| `emissions` | Calculated kg CO2e |
| `timestamp` | ISO 8601 string of the log time |
| `details` | Weight in grams (optional detail string) |

<Warning>
  There is no edit function for logged activities. If you enter the wrong weight, log the corrected entry and disregard the incorrect one in your history.
</Warning>
